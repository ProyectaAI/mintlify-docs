Once your Proyecta app is wrapped as a PWA and listed in an app store, updating it works in two tiers. Most of your day-to-day product work — new features, bug fixes, copy changes — ships instantly the moment you publish in the builder, with no review queue involved. Only a handful of changes require regenerating the native wrapper and going through store review again.

## Tier 1: Content and functionality updates (instant)

Because a wrapped PWA loads its content directly from your published Proyecta URL, changes to your app's behavior and appearance don't require a new store build.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Make your changes">
    Edit your app in the Proyecta builder as you normally would.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Publish">
    Click **Publish** in the builder toolbar to push your changes live.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Users see the update immediately">
    The next time a user opens the app, they get the updated version — no download or update prompt required.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Changes that ship instantly include:

- New pages and features
- Bug fixes
- Copy and image changes
- Backend logic and database schema changes
- Most styling tweaks

<Tip>
  This is one of the biggest advantages of the PWA-wrapper model. You're not waiting on App Store or Google Play review queues for normal product work.
</Tip>

## Tier 2: Native shell updates (new submission required)

Some changes are baked into the native wrapper itself and require you to regenerate the package and submit a new version to the store.

Changes that require a new submission:

- **App name** as it appears under the icon
- **App icon**
- **Splash screen**
- **Manifest entries** the wrapper bakes in (theme color, display mode, orientation lock)
- **Native permission declarations** (camera, location, or other permissions that need new entries in `Info.plist` or `AndroidManifest.xml`)
- **Wrapper version bumps**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Regenerate the package">
    Go to [pwabuilder.com](https://pwabuilder.com), enter your published URL, and generate a new native package.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign with your original key">
    Sign the package with the same signing key you used for the first submission. Using a different key will cause the submission to be rejected.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit for review">
    Upload through Google Play Console, App Store Connect, or Microsoft Partner Center and wait for review.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Protect your signing key

<Warning>
  For Android apps, you must sign every update with the same `signing.keystore` file used for the original submission. If you lose this file, Google Play will not accept further updates — you would have to publish a brand-new app under a different package name and ask users to migrate. Back up your keystore the moment you generate it.
</Warning>

The same applies to iOS provisioning profiles and Windows certificates. Store them somewhere safe and backed up, separate from your project files.

## Versioning tip

When you do submit a new native package, keep your app's user-facing version number in sync with your Proyecta deploy. This makes it much easier to match crash reports and user feedback to the correct build.
