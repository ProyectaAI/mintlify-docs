The **Commerce → Transactions** tab shows every charge tied to your app — with status, amount, currency, customer, and product references. From here you can issue refunds without leaving the builder.

## Transaction states

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `succeeded` | Payment captured successfully |
| `pending` | Awaiting capture or async confirmation |
| `failed` | Charge attempt failed |
| `refunded` | Fully refunded |
| `partially_refunded` | Some of the amount has been refunded |
| `disputed` | Customer has disputed the charge with their bank |

## Issue a refund

Click any transaction in the **Transactions** tab and choose **Refund**. You can refund the full amount or a partial amount. Select a reason when prompted:

- **Duplicate** — the customer was accidentally charged twice
- **Fraudulent** — the charge was not authorized by the customer
- **Requested by customer** — the customer asked for a refund

Refunds are processed through Stripe and credited back to the customer's original payment method. Typical settlement time is 5–10 business days.

## What's tracked per transaction

Each transaction record includes:

- `id` — unique identifier
- `customer_id` and `product_id` — the customer and product involved
- `amount` and `currency` — the charge amount and currency code
- `status` — current state (see states above)
- `refund_amount` — amount refunded, if any
- `created_at` — timestamp of the charge

## Disputes (chargebacks)

If a customer disputes a charge with their bank, the transaction status flips to `disputed`. Stripe handles the dispute response flow — you'll receive an email notification and can manage evidence submission through your Stripe Dashboard.

<Note>
A native dispute response flow inside the Proyecta builder is on the roadmap. For now, handle disputes directly in your Stripe Dashboard.
</Note>
