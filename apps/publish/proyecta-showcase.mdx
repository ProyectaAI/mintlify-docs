A public Proyecta Showcase — with community profiles, an explore page, and remixable templates — is planned for a future release. In the meantime, you can share any published app right now by sharing its URL. No showcase required.

## Share your app today

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Free subdomain" icon="globe" href="/apps/publish/proyecta-domain">
    Every published app gets a `yourapp.proyecta.dev` URL. Share it anywhere — social media, email, Slack, or as a QR code — and anyone with the link can open it instantly.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Custom domain" icon="link" href="/apps/publish/connect-existing-domain">
    Connect a domain you own for a stronger brand presence. See Custom Domains for options.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

If you want to give others a starting point to build from, Proyecta's template library is available today during project creation. The full remix and fork flow is part of the planned Showcase.

## Coming soon: the Proyecta Showcase

The public Showcase will add the following features:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Public profiles">
    A `proyecta.dev/@your-org` page that lists all of your organization's public apps in one place.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Explore page">
    A browsable gallery of apps built by the Proyecta community, organized by category.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Remixable templates">
    Let others clone your app as a starting point for their own projects. Only the app structure is cloned — your data is never shared.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Categories">
    Browse apps by type: SaaS, marketplace, internal tool, and more.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Rich showcase cards">
    Add up to six preview screenshots and a markdown description to your app's showcase listing.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Visibility and remix controls">
    Toggle public visibility and remixability on each app from its settings.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

Until the Showcase ships, a free subdomain and a shareable link is the fastest way to get eyes on what you've built.
