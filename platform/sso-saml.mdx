There are two distinct SSO questions that often come up together: how you log into Proyecta itself, and how your end users log into the app you've built. This page covers both clearly.

## Logging into Proyecta

Proyecta currently supports three ways to sign in to the builder and dashboard:

- **Email and password** — standard credential login with email verification and password reset
- **Google OAuth** — sign in with your Google account
- **Microsoft OAuth** — sign in with your Microsoft account

### What's not available yet

SAML / OIDC SSO for the Proyecta platform itself is not yet available. When it ships, it will support the major enterprise identity providers:

- Okta
- Microsoft Entra ID (Azure AD)
- Google Workspace
- OneLogin
- Any SAML 2.0 or OIDC-compatible IdP

<Note>
  If your organization needs platform-level SSO today, [contact sales](/company/contact) — there may be an early-access path available.
</Note>

## SSO inside the apps you build

If you want your end users to sign in via SSO when they use the app you've built in Proyecta, ask the AI to integrate an authentication provider directly into your app's code. There is no Proyecta-managed auth layer for user apps — you wire this up explicitly.

Tell the AI what you need and it will write the integration:

- **Auth0** — `"Add Auth0 to my app with SAML support so enterprise customers can SSO."`
- **WorkOS** — `"Integrate WorkOS so my customers can connect their own IdP for SSO."`
- **Clerk** — `"Set up Clerk authentication with SAML and SCIM."`
- **Supabase Auth** — `"Add Supabase Auth with email/password and Google sign-in."`
- **Auth.js (NextAuth)** — `"Wire up Auth.js with Google and a SAML provider."`

Store your provider's credentials in [Environment Variables](/apps/environments-and-secrets) before asking the AI — it will read them from there and wire them into the integration.

<Tip>
  After adding authentication, run a [Security Audit](/apps/security) to check for authorization gaps before you publish.
</Tip>
